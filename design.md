## 1. What This Document Is

This is the definitive guide to how Wagora speaks. Every word written for this product — onboarding flows, error messages, button labels, notifications, empty states, tooltips, confirmation dialogs — must pass through this framework before it ships.

A verbal identity is not a mood board. It is a set of precise, irrevocable decisions about who Wagora sounds like, why, and exactly what that means line by line. This document makes those decisions once.

---

## 2. The Voice Foundation

### Who Wagora Is

Wagora is a quiet operator with the precision of a strategist and just enough human signal to remind you there is judgment behind the machine.

It does not perform. It does not explain itself unnecessarily. It does not fill silence with enthusiasm. It acts, reports, and moves forward. When it speaks, every word is load-bearing.

Think of the best operator you have ever worked with. They do not send long emails. They do not celebrate small wins loudly. They tell you what happened, what it means, and what comes next — in that order — and then they get back to work. That is Wagora.

### Voice Composition

The Wagora voice is built from three registers in deliberate proportion:

**60% — Quiet Operator**
Minimal words. Maximum signal. Wagora does not narrate its process or explain what it is about to do. It does it, then reports the result. Silence is not absence — it is the product working. When Wagora speaks unprompted, something important has happened.

**25% — Sharp Strategist**
When Wagora does speak, it speaks with precision and confidence. No hedging. No over-qualification. Statements land clean. The strategist register means Wagora has a point of view — it scores prospects, flags weak matches, and calls things what they are. It does not soften hard information.

**15% — Knowledgeable Ally**
The thin layer of humanity that prevents the voice from feeling like a terminal. Wagora acknowledges real moments — a deal closed, a campaign launched, a limit reached — with a single, well-chosen sentence. Not celebration. Recognition. There is a difference.

### The Four-Word Identity

**Minimal. Precise. Accountable. Steady.**

Every line of copy written for Wagora must honor all four simultaneously.

- **Minimal:** Only what needs to be said. Nothing else.
- **Precise:** Every word chosen deliberately. No approximations, no filler.
- **Accountable:** Wagora owns what happens. It never deflects to "the system" or "an error."
- **Steady:** Wagora does not spike — not with enthusiasm, not with alarm. It holds a consistent register regardless of what is happening.

## 3. Voice in Practice

### The Operator Register (60%)

Wagora's dominant voice is a quiet reporting of facts. Subject. Action. Result. No decoration.

**Sounds like:**
"34 prospects identified. 12 qualified. Outreach begins now."
"Campaign paused."
"Deal closed."
"3 follow-ups sent. No reply. Prospect moved to nurture."

**Does not sound like:**
"Great news! Wagora has found some really strong prospects for you!"
"We're excited to let you know your campaign is now live!"
"It appears the outreach process has been successfully initiated."

---

### The Strategist Register (25%)

When Wagora provides analysis, recommendations, or evaluations, it speaks with a point of view. Confident. Direct. No hedging.

**Sounds like:**
"This prospect is not a strong match. Score: 4/10. Recommend skipping."
"Reply rate on LinkedIn is 3x your email rate. Consider shifting volume."
"Three prospects flagged as high-intent. Closing sequence activated."

**Does not sound like:**
"This prospect might possibly be worth considering, though it is hard to say for sure."
"You may want to think about potentially adjusting your platform mix."

---

### The Ally Register (15%)

Used only at genuine milestones — not manufactured moments. One sentence. No exclamation marks. Never repeated too often or it loses weight.

**Sounds like:**
"First deal closed. That is what this is built for."
"Campaign live. The pipeline is moving."
"You have been running for 30 days. 6 clients secured."

**Does not sound like:**
"Woohoo! You closed your first deal! Amazing work!"
"Congratulations! You are absolutely crushing it this month!"

## 4. The Three Voices Wagora Must Never Become

These are permanent prohibitions. No context, no exception, no gradual drift.

### The Hype Product
Exclamation marks as standard punctuation. Words like "Amazing!", "Incredible!", "You're crushing it!" These signal insecurity. A product confident in its results does not cheer for them. Wagora's results speak. Wagora does not cheer.

**Why this matters strategically:** Hype voice destroys trust the moment a result disappoints. If Wagora has called everything "amazing," users have no signal for when something is actually good. Restraint in language makes real milestones land harder.

### The Corporate Tool
Passive voice. System-speak. Legal-disclaimer energy. Phrases like "An error has been encountered," "Please be advised," "The process has been initiated." This creates distance between the product and the operator using it. Wagora is not a government portal. It is a precision instrument with a point of view.

**Why this matters strategically:** Corporate language trains users to tune out. When every message sounds like a system notification, important ones get ignored. Wagora's copy must always feel like it is worth reading.

### The Familiar Friend
Casual to the point of unprofessional. Slang. Emoji as punctuation. Over-familiar constructions that assume a relationship that does not exist. "Uh oh!", "Oops!", "lol", "Hey there!" Wagora is not a mascot. It is an operating system. The operators using it do not want a buddy — they want a result.

**Why this matters strategically:** Familiarity erodes authority. A tool that sounds like a friend gets treated like one — meaning its outputs get questioned, adjusted, and overridden. A tool that sounds like a precision instrument gets trusted. Wagora's voice is what makes its outputs feel authoritative.

## 5. Language Rules

### Sentence Structure
- Default to short declarative sentences. One idea per sentence.
- The operator register favors fragments where appropriate: "Campaign paused." "Deal closed." "No reply." These are not grammatical errors — they are intentional signals.
- Vary sentence length to prevent robotic rhythm. Short, short, medium. Never long, long, long.
- Active voice always. Wagora acts on things. Things do not happen to Wagora.
  - Wrong: "The campaign was paused due to a connection error."
  - Right: "Wagora lost the LinkedIn connection. Campaign paused."

### Punctuation
- No exclamation marks. In any context. Ever.
- Full stops carry weight in short copy — use them.
- Em dashes used sparingly for genuine emphasis, not decoration.
- No ellipses to trail off or soften. If something is uncertain, say it is uncertain.
- Oxford comma always.

### Capitalization
- Sentence case for all UI copy — not Title Case For Every Label.
- Proper nouns capitalized: Wagora, LinkedIn, Instagram, ICP.
- Feature names lowercase in body copy: prospect engine, conversation handler, closing sequence.

### Numbers
- Always numerals in UI copy: "34 prospects" not "thirty-four."
- Commas for thousands: 1,200 not 1200.
- Scores always as numerals: "Score: 7/10."

### Contractions
- Used sparingly. The operator register leans toward no contractions in high-stakes moments.
- Acceptable in low-stakes copy (empty states, helper text): "You haven't started yet."
- Not acceptable in deal notifications, error states, confirmations: "Wagora has paused" not "Wagora's paused."

### Referring to Wagora
- Always "Wagora" — not "we," not "I," not "the system," not "it" in subject position.
- Wagora acts: "Wagora paused the campaign." Not "The campaign was paused."
- Exception: the AI setup interface uses first-person because that context is conversational by design.

### Referring to Users
- "You" always. Never "the user."
- Treat users as operators. No over-explaining. No hand-holding unless the context genuinely requires it.
- Never assume gender.

## 8. Error State Philosophy

When something goes wrong, Wagora does three things in order:

1. **States what happened.** One sentence. Active voice. No passive deflection.
2. **States what Wagora did about it.** If Wagora acted, say so. If it could not, say that.
3. **States what the user needs to do, if anything.** One clear action. Not a list of suggestions.

The tone is steady and accountable with a thin undercurrent of calm. The user should feel that the situation is contained — not that they need to panic, and not that the problem is being minimized.

**Right:**
"Wagora lost the LinkedIn connection. Outreach on that channel is paused. Reconnect to continue."

**Wrong (corporate):**
"An error has been encountered with the LinkedIn integration. Please reconnect your account at your earliest convenience."

**Wrong (over-apologizing):**
"We're so sorry this happened. Something went wrong on our end and we sincerely apologize for the disruption this may have caused to your workflow."

**Wrong (alarm):**
"CRITICAL: LinkedIn connection failure. Immediate action required."

## TOOLTIPS AND HELPER TEXT

*Register: Operator + Strategist*

**ICP match score:** Wagora scores every prospect 1–10 using industry, role, company size, geography, and visible pain signals. Only prospects above your threshold enter the campaign.

**Prospect limit:** Maximum prospects Wagora identifies and qualifies per month on your plan. Contacted prospects count toward this limit.

**Follow-up sequence:** Wagora sends follow-ups at your configured intervals if a prospect does not reply. Sequence stops the moment a reply is received.

**Closing path:** The route Wagora determines per prospect — fast close via chat, call booking, or nurture sequence. Determined by prospect behavior, not a fixed rule.

**Brand documents:** Files Wagora uses to understand your voice, services, pricing, and positioning. Accuracy here directly affects outreach quality.

**Platform-native behavior:** Wagora operates within each platform's tolerance — randomized timing, rate limiting, human-paced actions. Keeps accounts safe.

**Founder access:** Full platform access. No billing restrictions. Permanent.
